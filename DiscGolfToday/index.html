Get Started

The best way to start is just getting a single driver and learning how to throw that single disc. If you are able to spend $50, you will be able to get three discs, two putters that are are same weight, and a driver. This will allow you to practice consistent putting techniques, as well as getting the feel for throwing your putter longer distances as well for increased accuracy in your short game. Only having one driver will give you an opportunity to practice your forehand and backhand drives and learn that discs flight pattern so that way you feel comfortable stepping up to the tee.

Different Types of Discs

There are Putters and Drivers (similar to ball golf), and there are also midranges, which will
take strokes off of your game and allow you to have a variety of consistent 200-350ft shots in your bag.
